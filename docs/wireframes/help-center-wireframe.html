Section: Security Spotlight

Your beans are
safe with us

beanies.family uses bank-grade encryption that runs entirely in your browser. Your data never touches our servers unencrypted — because it never touches our servers at all.

  • 🔐

    AES-256-GCM Encryption

    The same authenticated encryption used by governments and banks. Every byte of your family data is encrypted before it leaves memory.

  • 🔑

    PBKDF2 Key Derivation

    Your password is strengthened through 100,000 rounds of PBKDF2-SHA256 before it becomes an encryption key. Brute force isn't happening.

  • 👪

    Per-Member Key Wrapping

    Each family member has their own wrapped copy of the family key. Change your password without re-encrypting the entire file.

  • 💻

    Browser-Native Crypto

    All encryption uses the Web Crypto API built into your browser — no third-party libraries, no external dependencies, no supply chain risk.

How your data is protected
1 Your password + random salt
↓
2 PBKDF2 (100,000 rounds) → AES key
↓
3 AES-KW unwraps the family key
↓
4 Family key decrypts your .beanpod
All of this happens in your browser. Nothing is sent anywhere.

How your data is encrypted

Security 📖 8 min read Updated March 2026

beanies.family encrypts all of your family data using AES-256-GCM — the same authenticated encryption standard used by governments, banks, and security-critical applications worldwide. Here's exactly how it works, step by step.

🌱 The short version
Your password is turned into an encryption key. That key unwraps your family's master key. The master key decrypts your data. All of this happens in your browser — nothing is ever sent to a server.

Your password becomes a key

When you enter your password, beanies.family doesn't use it directly. Instead, it runs your password through PBKDF2 (Password-Based Key Derivation Function 2) with 100,000 rounds of SHA-256 hashing and a random 16-byte salt unique to your account.

This process is deliberately slow — it takes your browser about half a second. That's intentional. It means an attacker trying to guess your password would need half a second per guess, making brute-force attacks impractical.

💡 Why a salt matters

The random salt ensures that even if two family members choose the same password, their derived encryption keys will be completely different. It also prevents precomputed "rainbow table" attacks.

The family key

Your family's data isn't encrypted with your password directly. Instead, there's a single family key — a random 256-bit AES key generated when the pod was created. This key is what actually encrypts and decrypts your data.

Each family member holds a wrapped copy of this family key, protected by their own password-derived key using AES-KW (AES Key Wrap, RFC 3394). This means:

// Simplified view of the encryption layers

Your password
  ↓ PBKDF2(100,000 rounds, SHA-256, random salt)
Password-derived key
  ↓ AES-KW unwrap
Family key // shared by all members
  ↓ AES-256-GCM decrypt(12-byte IV)
Your family data // Automerge CRDT binary

What this means for you

This architecture gives you several powerful guarantees. Changing your password only re-wraps the family key — it doesn't re-encrypt your entire data file. Each encryption uses a fresh random IV, so even identical data produces different ciphertext. And the authentication tag in AES-GCM means tampering is detected immediately.

How is my net worth calculated?
🌱
Your net worth combines all account balances (checking, savings, investments) plus asset values, minus any loan balances. Each value is converted to your display currency using the latest exchange rates. The formula is:

Net Worth = Accounts + Assets − Loans
