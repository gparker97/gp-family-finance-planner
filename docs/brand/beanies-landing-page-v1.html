OUR STORY

Hey, guys!

Yes, it's actually me. Even AI could not come up with a greeting as mundane as "Hey, guys!" Indeed, there are still some things humans are better at than robots, such as glorious, unadulterated, deliciously spontaneous non-sequiturs, like how I like to fart outside taco trucks on wednesday afternoons. But getting back to the point...

🫘

Since the day I left (well, "graduated") college for my first real job, put in a solid month of (let's call it) work, and witnessed my first ever paycheck direct-deposited to my bank account, this is the app I've wanted to create. I gazed lovingly at that glorious $2,867 (minus taxes) and dreamt about the day I would definitely become a billionaire.

🫘

It started with a janky spreadsheet. I added formulas, then charts. I tried keeping track of how much I spent and saved each month and project that forward. I might have even written some newbie VB macros, and toyed with it for months (maybe years) trying to get it to work the way I wanted. Then, the world changed (in, let's say, 2008), and I said to myself, "Hey, self! Why do you keep trying to re-invent the wheel (the wheel having been invented earlier that year)? There's a million personal finance apps out there! Just download one, you numbskull."

🫘

So I did. I fiddled with those fickle banking connectors that try to sync your transactions, but always fail. Then they ask for your credentials 10 times in a row, and proceed to not work anyway. They ask you to upload CSVs, then say they can't read them. Lots of my important transactions were in cash or paid on cards. Some banks and cards don't provide reliable APIs. When things did work (rarely) it was temporary and only tracked what you did spend, not what you planned to spend.

My goal was never to track my budget down to the penny. If that's yours, this app probably isn't for you. I just wanted a rough idea, so I would know if I was going to end up as a hobo in 6 months.

🫘

Then, we had kids. With one (1), you can generally manage. 2 kids, life gets a bit harder. Bump that up to 3 (at school age) and welcome to the insane world of chaos and confusion and writing readmes to dumb personal family planning apps you vibe coded on a whim at 3am. Or, whatever.

Soccer practice, piano lessons, math tutor, gymnastics (2 types), karate, taekwondo, and don't forget your kid's actual school fees, your rent (or mortgage), and the list goes on. Who has to pay for what, and when, and how, and to who? Or is it whom? Also, while you were obsessing over the proper application of archaic English grammar rules, you realized you forgot to pick up your kid from his baseball tryouts. And it's raining. At least he has his umbrella. (Cue glance to the floor to spot the umbrella that fell out of his sports bag).

🫘

When my youngest was about 6 months old, my wife started calling him 小豆豆. Which in Chinese sounds endearing, but just means little bean. Cuz he sort of looked like one. It sounds cute in English too, so I called him beanie, and it stuck.

🫘

I built this app to track all that stuff I mentioned above: your money, your accounts, what you spent and received and plan to spend and receive (roughly), and to tell you how much you'll have in the future — in short, your "wealth".

But also, to help keep track of your actual family life: the kids' lessons, schoolwork, practice, family vacations (we may even give you advice), and, of course, who will pick up whom (yes, whom). In short, anything we can help you with, even in a small way, as you navigate the joys and sorrows of a complicated, crowded, chaotic, stressful, but of course, rewarding? (sorry, I meant, rewarding) family life.

🫘

It's not a perfect app. It doesn't sync with banks (and I doubt it ever will). But it gives you a rough idea of how much you have, how much you will have, and what in the world you actually need to do today. Of course, only if you tell it that stuff first. It's not magic.

Treat beanies.family like a friend, and it will reward you in spades. Maybe it will even buy you a beer the next time you have 22 minutes free after work and $7 saved from meticulously tracking your budget every month. (Is that how much beers cost nowadays? I wouldn't know).

🫘

You can clone and install it for free if you want to run it locally. The data never leaves your machine if you don't want it to. We don't even own a database (well, not a real one). I don't want your janky finance data anyway, and I'm not picking up your kid for you.

If you want to use the cloud version so you can access it from the internet and easily share it with your family (don't worry, your data still stays with you), create an account at beanies.family. You could also set up the infra by yourself, but that's a pain and you know it. You can try it for free (that's how we get you). But hey, you can swing a few bucks (although I wouldn't know, as I can't read your data). But, you know, we have bills to pay, too.

🫘

Here's the fun stuff:

🤖 Claude Max / Opus 4.6
⚡ Vue / TypeScript / Tailwind / Pinia
💾 IndexedDB
🔐 Web Crypto API
🧪 Vitest + Playwright + Dependabot
☁️ One single, stupid, plain vanilla DynamoDB

One single, stupid, plain vanilla DynamoDB in the cloud to map your email to family ID so we can locate your (encrypted) family data file, that only you can access. Nothing else. Anywhere. Ever.

So there it is. If you're a techie nerd like me, run it locally. If you don't want to deal with all the bs, use it on the cloud.

🫘

I'll be using beanies.family to keep track of our money, our various (1) houses, boats (currently at 0), and diamond rings (ask my wife where she lost hers.. unfortunately we can't tell you that), and more. I'll also use it for their piano lessons, soccer practices, and to make sure we don't forgot to pick up our son from his after school math tutor (again). All while keeping that data safe and secure (unless I open read access on my data file to the world and tell everyone the key. Which I won't. Probably.).

If you have a question, comment, suggestion, or just wanna say hi, you can say it here or raise an issue. If you're using it for free and wanna drop me a satoshi for my time (or just cuz you like the app), that's cool too.

Simplify your family life. Every bean counts.

Enjoy beanies, bros.

Peace.

Greg

What beanies does
Two sides. One app.
The Piggy Bank handles your money. The Treehouse handles your life. Together, they keep your family on track.
🐷
The Piggy Bank
Track accounts, income, expenses — recurring and one-time. See your net worth grow over time. No bank sync needed, just honest numbers.
🌳
The Treehouse
Activities, schedules, to-dos, and family milestones. Know who's going where, when, and who's picking them up. Finally.
🔮
Future You
Project forward. See what your finances look like in 3, 6, 12 months based on your recurring income and expenses. No surprises.
🔒
Your Data, Your Rules
End-to-end encrypted. Run it locally or in the cloud. We literally cannot read your data. And we don't want to.
